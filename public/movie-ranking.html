The Best Movies According To Abbu

My top 2 movies of all-time.

1. MY NAME IS KHAN(2010)

This movie has a special place in my heart.

The film explores the life of Rizwan Khan, impeccably portrayed by Shah Rukh Khan, a man with Asperger's syndrome, as he embarks on a journey to convey a powerful message of unity and love. Khan's performance is nothing short of extraordinary, showcasing his versatility as an actor and his ability to immerse himself fully into the complex character he potrays.

Kajol, who plays the role of Mandira, delivers an exceptional performance, bringing depth and authenticity to her character. The chemistry between Shah Rukh Khan and Kajol is palpable and adds a layer of believability and emotional resonance to the film.

The cinematography in "My Name is Khan" is exquisite, capturing the beauty of diverse landscapes and cultures. The film seamlessly transitions from the bustling streets of San Francisco to the serenity of small-town America, enhancing the narrative's authenticity and emotional impact.

One of the film's strengths lies in its ability to evoke a range of emotions, from joy and laughter to heart-wrenching sorrow. The music, composed by Shankar-Ehsaan-Loy, complements the narrative beautifully, enhancing the emotional connection with the characters and their experiences.

At its core, "My Name is Khan" is a film about love, acceptance, and the power of standing up for what is right. It serves as a reminder that, regardless of our differences, we are all connected by our humanity and should strive to foster compassion and understanding in a world that often seems divided.

2. HIROMIYA(2021)

Horimiya, an anime adapted from the popular manga by HERO, is a delightful journey into the world of genuine human connections, beautifully brought to life on the screen. This series is a breath of fresh air in the romance and slice-of-life genres, offering a unique and heartwarming perspective on friendship, self-discovery, and love.

The story revolves around the seemingly ordinary high school lives of Kyoko Hori and Izumi Miyamura, two characters whose hidden complexities and true personalities are revealed when they discover each other's secrets. Their evolving relationship is at the core of the series, showcasing the transformative power of understanding and acceptance.

The chemistry between Hori and Miyamura is portrayed with sincerity and authenticity, evoking a genuine sense of warmth and tenderness. Their interactions are imbued with honesty and vulnerability, reinforcing the message that true love and friendship stem from accepting one another without judgment.
The musical score in Horimiya complements the narrative beautifully, enhancing the emotional resonance of the scenes. The opening and ending themes are catchy and memorable, adding to the overall charm of the series.

In conclusion, Horimiya stands as a testament to the power of authenticity and the beauty of human connection. It is a must-watch for anyone seeking a heartwarming, realistic portrayal of friendship and love in the midst of life's ups and downs. This anime offers a refreshing take on the complexities of adolescence, leaving a lasting impression on the hearts of its viewers.
